The Great Migration

A story of self-determination

Here is a time line of some important events from the Great Migration:

  • 1900- more than 90% of the Black population in the U.S. lives in the American South
  • 1910 to 1930- the Black population increases by 40% in major U.S. cities.
  • 1930s and 1940s- Many Black farmers are forced off of land in the South due to mechnanization of farming. More than 1 million Black southerners move to cities in the North and West as a result.
  • 1950s- 1.1 million more Black southerners move to cities in the North and West.
  • 1960s and 19702- Continuing the trend, 2.4 million Black southerners move to cities in the North and West.
  • late 1970s- Deindustrialization in the United States effectively brings the Great Migration to an end, as job opportunities decline in U.S. cities.
  • 1970- more than 80% of Black Americans live in cities.
